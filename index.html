Examples - How Climate Change Hurts Animals

Polar Bears

As temperatures rise, Arctic sea ice is melting earlier in the year and refreezing later. Polar bears are losing the sea ice they need to hunt for seals. Without ice, they struggle to find enough food and often starve. Many polar bear populations are shrinking.

Penguins

In Antarctica, penguins experience the same challenges. Emperor penguins depend on stable ice to raise their offsprings and to breed. As the ice melts sooner, numerous young penguins are left without a place to survive. The resulting smaller breeding area also increases the pressure on their population.

Seals

Seals also suffer. Many seals give birth on ice platforms. Without stable ice, the young seals are more vulnerable to predation and death by drowning and starvation. As these animals suffer, the entire food chain in polar regions will become unstable.

Their Struggles Matter to Us

Katharine Hayhoe makes the point that many people consider global warming to be something apart from their own lives. Polar bears live thousands of miles away and appear to have no relationship to their everyday life. Therefore, it's easy for people to think the melting Arctic and Antarctic ice endangers only the creatures living there and has negligible impact on the lives of humans. However, what those animals are struggling with is actually representative of greater issues that have a direct impact on people around the world. The polar regions are key to the Earth's climate system. The melting sea ice raises sea levels and puts cities along the coastlines and communities further at risk.

In addition to rising sea levels, polar ecosystem changes also impact global food supplies. Human food resources rely heavily on many of the fish found in polar food webs, and when the food webs break down, it reduces our food supply and damages the local economies. The same forces of the climate causing the melting of Arctic ice also bring more intense heat waves, larger storms, and changing weather patterns to many locations worldwide. With the warming Arctic and Antarctic, the ocean currents and weather patterns change, resulting in longer droughts, more intense floods, and more intense hurricanes. The struggles of these animals are not separate from our own, instead, they are warning signs of bigger problems that affect both nature and human life.

Governments can enforce tighter regulations to protect the environment and reduce pollution. Businesses can move towards clean power and green practices, such as wind and solar energy.
